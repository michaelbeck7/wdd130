Overview

Purpose

Think about how you keep track of all your recipes right now: is it a cookbook stored away somewhere? An overflowing folder that you have to dig through every time a holiday comes around and you need? What if you could access all of your recipes in one place from your phone, or computer. That’s what my website would be: a single place to store all my recipes whether you're grocery shopping, cooking, or planning my meals for the week.

Audience

The members of the target audience of this website are busy college students looking for easy recipes. They are people who enjoy cooking. Because they are busy and looking to maximize every minute, they expect a site that is simple, clean, and presents recipes without extra fluff. Becaues our audience will be using both phones and laptops, the website must be responsive and easy to navigate on both mobile and desktop.

Shopping Tips

Moving away from home and starting college comes with a host of new responsibilities. Along with attending classes, studying, completing assignments, and washing laundry, one new responsibility can be especially daunting: cooking meals. Students must learn how to plan nutritious and satisfying meals for their week, and how to stock their shelves on a budget. Here are some tips to get you started. Meal prep during the weekend to save time during the rest of the week. Find a meal that will be enjoyable enough to eat all week, and cook up enough to pack in the fridge or freezer for quick reheating later. This practice will give more time for studying during the week and decreases the temptation to spend more money eating out. One good option for meal prep is rice/protein/veggies. Take your pick of white rice or brown rice. Then, cook up your favorite protein. Some options include chicken, beef, or beans if you prefer vegetarian. Then finish it off with your choice of veggies. Microwave-able steamed frozen vegetables such as broccoli or mixed vegetables are a convenient way to go. Top it off with your favorite sauce, pack it up in the fridge, and you’ll be set for the rest of the week.
